Sydney's e-commerce market is competitive. Fashion, homewares, beauty, specialty retail — most categories have three or four credible players fighting for the same customer, and acquisition costs have been climbing for years.

In that environment, email is one of the few channels where the economics still work clearly in your favour. It costs the same to send to 10,000 subscribers whether you're a $2m brand or a $20m one. The difference is what you send, who you send it to, and how well the automation runs in the background.

## What we build and manage

**Lifecycle flows.** The automations that run continuously — welcome, abandoned browse, abandoned cart, post-purchase, win-back. Built with correct trigger logic, audience exclusions, and tested copy. Tuned monthly against revenue data, not opens.

**Campaign calendar.** A planned monthly calendar of broadcast campaigns — segmented, written, designed, and sent. Promotions, launches, content sends. We own the cadence so your list stays warm and the sends are worth receiving.

**Deliverability.** SPF, DKIM, and DMARC set up and enforcing. List hygiene on a regular cycle. Sender reputation monitoring. Sydney brands with strong e-commerce programs often discover that their deliverability setup has been quietly limiting reach — it's worth auditing.

**Reporting.** Monthly reporting built around revenue per recipient and email's contribution to total revenue. Delivered with the context to act on it, not just the numbers.

## Why it matters to get this right

Most Sydney e-commerce brands are spending significant budget on paid social and search — Meta, Google, TikTok. That acquisition spend only compounds if the lifecycle is strong enough to convert new subscribers and keep existing customers buying.

Email is the connective tissue. A customer who buys once and receives nothing meaningful is as good as a lost customer. A customer who gets a well-timed post-purchase sequence, relevant campaign content, and an occasional win-back attempt when they go quiet has a materially different LTV.

That's the gap we close.
