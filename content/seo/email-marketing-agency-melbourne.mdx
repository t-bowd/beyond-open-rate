Melbourne has a strong creative and business culture, and its e-commerce market reflects that — fashion, design, food and beverage, professional services, and a growing SaaS sector. The brands that do well tend to have a clear point of view and a customer relationship worth investing in.

Email, done well, is how you sustain that relationship between purchases.

## What we do

**Lifecycle automation.** The always-on flows that earn revenue without ongoing input — welcome series, abandoned browse, abandoned cart, post-purchase sequences, win-back campaigns. Built correctly once, then tested and tuned against revenue data.

**Campaign management.** A monthly campaign calendar that keeps your list engaged and drives the short-term revenue peaks — launches, promotions, seasonal sends, editorial content. We write, design, segment, and send. You approve the plan.

**Platform setup and migration.** If you're on the wrong platform — or the right platform but set up incorrectly — we handle the configuration or the migration. We work primarily with Klaviyo for e-commerce and Brevo, ActiveCampaign, and HubSpot for services and SaaS businesses.

**Deliverability.** A lot of Melbourne brands have invested in building a list and then quietly undermined their own reach by neglecting authentication, letting unengaged contacts accumulate, or varying their send volume erratically. We fix that.

**Reporting.** Monthly reporting on revenue per recipient, contribution to total revenue, and flow-level performance. The metrics that tell you whether the program is working — not whether people clicked.

## The compounding case for email

Paid channels have a ceiling: when you stop spending, the traffic stops. Email compounds. A subscriber who joins your list today and goes through a well-built welcome series, receives relevant campaigns over the following months, and gets a well-timed win-back when they lapse is worth significantly more than a subscriber who joined the same list and received nothing meaningful.

That compounding effect takes time to build — but the earlier you build the infrastructure correctly, the sooner it starts.
