## Variant A

You didn't get unlucky with that email agency.

You got exactly what the engagement was designed to deliver.

Three months of sends. Weekly or bi-weekly campaigns. A wrap report with open rates, click rates, maybe some A/B test results if you were lucky.

And at the end of it, no clear answer to: "Did this make us money?"

That's not because the agency was lazy or incompetent. It's because the engagement wasn't structured around revenue. It was structured around activity.

Sends per month. Campaigns delivered. Opens and clicks tracked.

All of which can go up while your email channel contributes less to the business than it did before you hired them.

The problem isn't agencies. The problem is how most email engagements are sold and scoped.

If the success metric is campaign volume or engagement rate, you'll get campaign volume and engagement rate. You won't get clarity on whether email is pulling its weight as a revenue channel.

Most founders who've been burned once don't try again. They go back to doing it in-house or let email sit on autopilot.

Which means the problem that made them hire someone in the first place — email leaking money — never actually gets fixed.

## Variant B

The report had 11 pages.

Graphs showing open rates trending up. Click-through rates by campaign. Heatmaps of where people clicked in the emails.

You nodded through the call. It looked impressive. It felt like work had been done.

And then you looked at your P&L and realised you had no idea if any of it mattered.

That's what "burned by an agency" usually means. Not that they didn't do the work. They probably did. They sent the campaigns. They optimised subject lines. They ran A/B tests.

But the engagement was never tied to the thing you actually care about.

Revenue from email as a channel. Repeat purchase rate. How much cart abandonment you're recovering. Whether lifecycle is increasing LTV or just adding noise.

If the agency's KPIs are open rates and click rates, that's what you'll get. And those numbers can look great while email contributes less and less to your bottom line.

This isn't about finding a "better" agency. It's about how the engagement is structured in the first place.

If success isn't defined as revenue impact, you'll never know if the work mattered.

And most email engagements aren't structured that way. Which is why most founders who try once don't try again.
