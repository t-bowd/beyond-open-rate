## Variant A

Your agency sends you a monthly email report.

Page one: open rates are up 3.2%.
Page two: click rates improved by 1.8%.
Page three: engagement is trending positive.

Nowhere in that report does it say how much money email made you last month.

Because they don't measure it.

They measure activity. Opens. Clicks. Forwards. Time of day. Device type.

All of it is irrelevant if you don't know whether the emails generated more revenue than they cost to send.

We report one number first: revenue per recipient.

Not revenue per open. Not revenue per click. Revenue per person on the list.

If that number goes up month-over-month, email is working. If it goes down, it's not. Everything else is just commentary.

Opens tell you the subject line worked. Clicks tell you the copy worked. Revenue tells you the business worked.

Most agencies report the first two because they're easier to move and harder to be held accountable for.

We report the third because that's the only one that matters.

## Variant B

You sent 50,000 emails last month.

5,000 people opened them. That's a 10% open rate. Your agency called it a win.

But here's what they didn't tell you:

Those 50,000 emails generated $8,400 in revenue.

That's $0.17 per recipient.

The top-performing e-commerce brands we work with generate $2–$4 per recipient. Some months higher.

You're not underperforming because your open rate is too low.

You're underperforming because you're measuring the wrong thing.

Open rate measures visibility. Revenue per recipient measures whether email is doing its job.

And its job isn't to get opened. Its job is to make you money.

When you shift to revenue per recipient as your primary metric, everything changes.

You stop optimising for clicks and start optimising for buying behaviour. You stop celebrating 15% open rates on emails that generated $300. You start killing campaigns that look good on paper but don't move the number that matters.

We don't report open rates in our monthly reviews. We report recovered revenue, repeat purchase rate, and revenue per recipient.

Because you didn't hire us to send emails. You hired us to grow the business.
