## Variant A

Email attribution for most DTC brands sits between 3% and 7%.

That number feels low. But it's not surprising when you look at what's actually being sent.

A welcome series. Maybe some campaigns. Cart abandonment if they're lucky.

Brands with proper lifecycle programs — the ones running win-back sequences, post-purchase nurture, VIP segmentation, replenishment reminders — see 20–30% of revenue attributed to email.

That's not a 5% improvement. It's a 4x multiple.

And the gap compounds.

Every month without a lifecycle program is a month of second purchases that didn't happen. Third purchases that were never triggered. Customers who lapsed at 90 days instead of being pulled back at 60.

You're not just losing this month's revenue. You're losing the compounding value of customers who would've stayed longer.

Most founders know email could be better. They don't realise it's a structural problem that gets worse with time.

## Variant B

What percentage of your revenue comes from email?

If you don't know the number off the top of your head, it's probably under 5%.

Here's what that means.

A customer buys once. You send them an order confirmation. Maybe a review request. Then nothing until your next campaign blast.

They don't buy again.

Brands with lifecycle email in place — proper post-purchase sequences, browse and cart abandonment, win-back flows, replenishment triggers — attribute 20–30% of total revenue to email.

The difference isn't the tool. It's the system.

And the gap gets wider every month you don't fix it.

Because lifecycle email doesn't just drive this month's revenue. It increases the chance a customer buys again next month. And the month after that.

Without it, you're starting from zero with every customer. Burning acquisition budget to replace the ones who didn't come back.

The cost isn't what you're spending. It's what you're not keeping.
