## Variant A

You set up three welcome emails two years ago.

Email 1: discount code.
Email 2: social proof.
Email 3: founder story.

Then nothing.

You haven't touched it since because it "works" — 18% open rate, couple of sales a week, no complaints.

But here's what you don't see in those dashboard stats:

The person who opens email one, clicks nothing, then opens email two three days later. They're warming up. They're still deciding. Email three lands. They read it. Then silence from you for two weeks until they get lumped into a generic broadcast about a sale.

The decision to buy usually happens between email five and email seven.

Not email one. Not email two.

Somewhere in that window after the introduction, after the discount pressure, when they're actually figuring out if your product fits their life.

Most brands go quiet exactly when the conversation should be getting specific.

Three emails isn't a welcome series. It's an introduction you never followed up on.

The brands recovering 60–70% more revenue from new subscribers aren't sending better emails in the first three. They're sending emails four through nine that you're not.

## Variant B

You're losing about 40% of the revenue you could be getting from new email subscribers.

Not because your welcome emails are bad.

Because they stop too early.

Here's what actually happens:

Day 1: Welcome email. 10% convert immediately. These people were already going to buy.

Day 3: Email two. Another 5% convert. Low-hanging fruit.

Day 5: Email three. Maybe 3% more.

Then you stop.

But the other 82% are still on your list. Still opening. Still deciding.

The real decision window is day 7 to day 21. That's when someone goes from "interesting brand" to "I'm going to buy this" or "not for me."

Most brands are silent during that entire window.

They've moved on. Sent the discount. Told the story. Done the social proof. Now those subscribers just get whatever broadcast you send to everyone else.

Meanwhile, the brands that extend their welcome series to eight or nine emails — with the right content, not just more discounts — are converting 18–24% of new subscribers instead of 11–13%.

Same traffic. Same acquisition cost. Nearly double the return.

The three-email welcome flow wasn't a strategy. It was a Shopify template everyone copied and never questioned.
