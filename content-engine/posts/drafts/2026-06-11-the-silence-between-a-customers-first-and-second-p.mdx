## Variant A

47 days.

That's the average gap between a customer's first and second purchase in e-commerce.

You spent $30 on Meta ads to acquire them.

They bought once.

Then nothing.

For 47 days, you go silent. No email. No reminder that you exist. No reason to come back.

Meanwhile, they're seeing retargeting ads from three of your competitors. Daily.

By day 20, they've forgotten your brand name.

By day 35, they've bought from someone else.

You didn't lose them because your product was bad. You lost them because silence is expensive and you let a competitor fill it.

Most brands track acquisition cost obsessively and repeat purchase rate not at all.

The gap between purchase one and purchase two is where profit lives or dies. If you're not filling those 47 days with something deliberate, someone else is.
