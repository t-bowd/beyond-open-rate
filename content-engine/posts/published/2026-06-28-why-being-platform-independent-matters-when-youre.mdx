## Variant A

Most email agencies make $300–$600 per client per month just by referring them to Klaviyo.

That's before they bill a single hour.

So when they tell you Klaviyo is the best platform for your business, ask yourself: are they recommending what's right for you, or what's right for them?

The truth is there's no universal best platform.

If you're doing $2M with 50,000 subscribers, Klaviyo might be expensive overkill. If you're doing $15M with complex segmentation needs, it might be exactly right. If you're on Shopify Plus and need tight integration with customer data, maybe it's Omnisend. Or Klaviyo. Or Sendlane.

It depends on your business.

Your order volume. Your list size. Your tech stack. Your team's technical ability. What you're actually trying to do with email beyond "send more of it."

A good agency doesn't have a house platform.

They ask questions first. They look at your data. They evaluate cost per send against what you're actually getting. Then they recommend the platform that fits.

If the first thing an agency does is migrate you to their preferred tool, you're not working with an agency. You're working with a reseller.

Worth asking before you sign anything.

## Variant B

You hire an email agency to grow revenue.

Three weeks later you're neck-deep in a platform migration you didn't ask for.

Your existing flows are being rebuilt from scratch. Your team is learning a new dashboard. You're fielding Slack messages about DNS records and sender authentication.

None of this is making you money.

Here's what happened: the agency you hired only works in one platform. So the first thing they do — before they touch a flow, before they look at your data — is move you to the tool they know.

It's not malicious. It's just how they operate.

But it means the first 4–6 weeks of the engagement are spent on migration work, not revenue work. And if the platform they've moved you to doesn't suit your business model, you've just burned time and budget solving the wrong problem.

The right platform depends on your business.

Your subscriber count. Your average order value. How technically capable your team is. Whether you need deep integration with your fulfilment system or just reliable delivery.

An agency that's genuinely platform-independent will evaluate this before recommending anything. They'll work with what you've got if it's fit for purpose. And if a migration makes sense, they'll tell you why — with numbers, not sales copy.

If an agency can't work in your current platform, they're not the right fit. Simple as that.
